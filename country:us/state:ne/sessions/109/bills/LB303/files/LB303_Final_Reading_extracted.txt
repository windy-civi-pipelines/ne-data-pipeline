1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to education; to amend section 79-1028.01,
2 Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to create the School Financing
3 Review Commission; to provide duties; to change provisions relating
4 to the ability of a school district to exceed its budget authority
5 for the general fund budget of expenditures under the Tax Equity and
6 Educational Opportunities Support Act; to repeal the original
7 section; and to declare an emergency.
8 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,

1 Section 1. (1) The School Financing Review Commission is created.
2 The commission shall consist of eighteen members, including:
3 (a) The Commissioner of Education or a designee of the Commissioner
4 of Education;
5 (b) The Property Tax Administrator or a designee of the Property Tax
6 Administrator;
7 (c) One representative of the Governor appointed by the Governor;
8 (d) Three members of the Legislature, who shall be nonvoting members
9 of the commission, including the Chairperson of the Education Committee
10 of the Legislature or the chairperson's designee, the Chairperson of the
11 Revenue Committee of the Legislature or the chairperson's designee, and
12 one member appointed pursuant to subsection (2) of this section. No more
13 than two members shall be affiliated with the same political party;
14 (e) One member who is a representative of postsecondary education
15 with expertise in school finance;
16 (f) Four members who are superintendents or school board members
17 representing a Class I, II, or III school district. There shall be at
18 least one member appointed pursuant to this subdivision (1)(f)
19 representing each class of school district. Of the members appointed
20 pursuant to this subdivision (1)(f), no more than two members shall
21 represent the same class of school district, no more than three members
22 may be superintendents, and no more than three members may be school
23 board members;
24 (g) One member that is a representative of a Class IV school
25 district and one member that is a representative of a Class V school
26 district. One member appointed pursuant to this subdivision (1)(g) shall
27 be a school board member of the school district being represented and the
28 other member appointed pursuant to this subdivision (1)(g) shall be a
29 school administrator in the other school district being represented; and
30 (h) Five members appointed from the state at large, which shall
31 include (i) at least one individual having experience in business, (ii)

2 individual teaching as a certified teacher in a public school, and (iv)

4 (ii) of this subdivision (1)(h) may be retired.

6 of this section shall be appointed by the Executive Board of the
7 Legislative Council in January of each odd-numbered year for a two-year
8 term, except that the initial member shall be appointed within thirty
9 days after the effective date of this act and shall serve until a
10 successor is appointed in January 2027.

16 shall serve terms as described in subsection (4) of this section.

18 vacancy shall be filled by the Governor for the remainder of the vacated
19 term. For purposes of determining term length and expiration of such
20 terms, the initial terms for such members shall begin thirty days after
21 the effective date of this act.

31 years; and

3 serve an initial term of one year.

5 for the members appointed subject to subdivision (4)(a) of this section.
6 (5) If at any point a member no longer meets the qualification for
7 such member's position on the commission, such member shall vacate
8 membership on the commission. Any vacancy in the commission shall be
9 filled in the same manner and same qualification as the original
10 appointment.
11 (6) A member of the commission appointed pursuant to subsection (3)
12 of this section shall not receive any compensation for services as a
13 member but shall be reimbursed for expenses incurred as a member of the
14 commission as provided in sections 81-1174 to 81-1177.
15 (7) The Commissioner of Education or the designee of the
16 Commissioner of Education shall be the chairperson of the commission, and
17 the commission shall elect a vice-chairperson from among its members.
18 (8) The commission shall meet at least four times a year and at
19 least once per calendar quarter.
20 (9) The State Department of Education may provide staff to assist
21 the commission as needed and requested by the chairperson of the
22 commission in accordance with guidelines developed by the commission. The
23 commission may also obtain assistance from the State Department of
24 Education, the Department of Revenue, and the Legislative Fiscal Analyst
25 in acquiring the data needed to carry out its duties.
26 (10) For administrative purposes, the commission shall be housed
27 within the State Department of Education.
28 Sec. 2. (1)(a) The purpose of the School Financing Review
29 Commission is to study and provide advisory recommendations relating to
30 school funding in Nebraska. The commission shall:
31 (i) Evaluate the school funding formula for education under the Tax

1 Equity and Educational Opportunities Support Act and make recommendations
2 for adjustments or changes to such formula that may be necessary to help
3 prevent the increase of property taxes;
4 (ii) Regularly review the resources side of the formula under the
5 Tax Equity and Educational Opportunities Support Act;
6 (iii) Regularly review the needs side of the formula under the Tax
7 Equity and Educational Opportunities Support Act;
8 (iv) Analyze options for how to minimize the negative impact on
9 schools during times when the state may be unable to fully fund the
10 formula under the Tax Equity and Educational Opportunities Support Act;
11 (v) Analyze the impact of funding for education on student
12 achievement and outcomes, focusing on, but not limited to: Access to
13 early childhood education; student attendance; literacy; graduation rate;
14 college-going rate; and postsecondary workforce participation; and
15 (vi) Analyze the impact of funding for education on issues and
16 resources impacting students with relatively high needs, including, but
17 not limited to, students who live in areas of high concentrations of
18 poverty, students with an individualized education program, and students
19 with limited English proficiency.
20 (b) The School Financing Review Commission, to the extent it is
21 practical, shall compare findings from subdivision (1)(a) of this section
22 to similar policies from other states.
23 (2) By December 1, 2025, and by November 1 of each year thereafter,
24 the commission shall prepare and electronically deliver to the Clerk of
25 the Legislature an annual report with the observations and
26 recommendations developed pursuant to subsection (1) of this section.
