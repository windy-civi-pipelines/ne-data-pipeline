14 Sec. 17. (1) Practice of occupational therapy means the therapeutic
15 use of everyday life occupations with persons, groups, or populations to
16 support occupational performance and participation. Occupational therapy
17 practice includes clinical reasoning and professional judgment to
18 evaluate, analyze, and diagnose occupational challenges and provide
19 occupation-based interventions. Occupational therapy services include
20 habilitation, rehabilitation, and the promotion of physical and mental
21 health and wellness for clients with all levels of ability-related needs.
22 These services are provided for clients who have or are at risk for
23 developing an illness, injury, disease, disorder, condition, impairment,
24 disability, activity limitation, or participation restriction. Through
25 the provision of skilled services and engagement in everyday activities,
26 occupational therapy promotes physical and mental health and well-being
27 by supporting occupational performance in a person with, or at risk of
28 experiencing a range of developmental, physical, and mental health
29 disorders.
