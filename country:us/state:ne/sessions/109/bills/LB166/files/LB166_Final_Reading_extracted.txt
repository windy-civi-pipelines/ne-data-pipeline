LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA
ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST SESSION
FINAL READING
Introduced by Hughes, 24; Raybould, 28.
Read first time January 13, 2025
Committee: Government, Military and Veterans Affairs
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to public records; to amend sections 23-3211
2 and 24-814, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, and section
3 32-607, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement, 2024; to require the
4 county treasurer to maintain confidentiality relating to residential
5 addresses of certain persons; to prohibit the disclosure of certain
6 information filed with the Secretary of State relating to judicial
7 officeholders; to change provisions relating to candidate filing
8 forms; and to repeal the original sections.
9 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB166 LB166
2025 2025
1 Section 1. Section 23-3211, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 23-3211 (1) Unless requested in writing, the county assessor, and
4 register of deeds, and county treasurer shall withhold from the public
5 the residential address of a law enforcement officer, a member of the
6 Nebraska National Guard acting pursuant to subdivision (3) of section
7 55-182, or a judge who applies to the county assessor in the county of
8 his or her residence. The application shall be in a form prescribed by
9 the county assessor and shall include the applicant's name and address
10 and the parcel identification number for his or her residential address.
11 A law enforcement officer shall include in the application his or her law
12 enforcement identification number. A member of the Nebraska National
13 Guard shall include in the application proof of his or her status as a
14 member, in a manner prescribed by the county assessor. A judge shall
15 include in the application the name of the specific court of which he or
16 she is a judge. The county assessor shall notify the register of deeds
17 and county treasurer regarding the receipt of a complete application. The
18 county assessor, and the register of deeds, and county treasurer shall
19 withhold the address of a law enforcement officer, member of the Nebraska
20 National Guard, or judge who complies with this section for five years
21 after receipt of a complete application. The officer, member, or judge
22 may renew his or her application every five years upon submission of an
23 updated application.
24 (2) For purposes of this section, judge includes the judge or
25 magistrate judge of any court located in this state, including any county
26 court, any district court, the Court of Appeals, the Nebraska Workers'
27 Compensation Court, any separate juvenile court, the Supreme Court, and
28 any federal court.
29 Sec. 2. Section 24-814, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
30 amended to read:
31 24-814 (1) Any judicial officeholder, subject to the terms of
-2-
LB166 LB166
2025 2025
1 sections 24-813 to 24-818, who desires to continue in office for an
2 additional term, shall indicate his or her desire in this respect in
3 writing filed with the Secretary of State, on or before August 1
4 immediately preceding the expiration of his or her term in office, and
5 shall request in writing that the Secretary of State submit to the
6 electorate of the appropriate district or area, the question of his or
7 her right to be retained in office for an additional term.
8 (2) Any written request filed with the Secretary of State shall not
9 be a public record pursuant to section 84-712.01 and shall not be subject
10 to disclosure under sections 84-712 to 84-712.09 or any other provision
11 of law, except that the Secretary of State shall electronically publish a
12 list, that includes the judicial officeholder's name and respective
13 district or area of the state served, of judicial officeholders who have
14 filed for retention in that calendar year.
15 Sec. 3. Section 32-607, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement,
16 2024, is amended to read:
17 32-607 (1)(a) All candidate filing forms shall contain the following
18 statement: I hereby swear that I will abide by the laws of the State of
19 Nebraska regarding the results of the primary and general elections, that
20 I am a registered voter and qualified to be elected, and that I will
21 serve if elected. Candidate filing forms shall also contain the following
22 information regarding the candidate: Name, as provided under subdivision
23 (b) of this subsection; residence address; mailing address if different
24 from the residence address; telephone number; office sought; party
25 affiliation if the office sought is a partisan office; a statement as to
26 whether or not civil penalties are owed pursuant to the Nebraska
27 Political Accountability and Disclosure Act; and, if civil penalties are
28 owed, whether or not a surety bond has been filed pursuant to subdivision
29 (4)(b) of section 32-602. An email address shall also be included on the
30 filing form as an optional field.
31 (b) The name contained on a candidate filing form shall be the name
-3-
LB166 LB166
2025 2025
1 by which the candidate is generally known in the community and by which
2 the candidate is distinguished from others and shall not contain titles,
3 characterizations, or designations.
4 (2) Candidate filing forms shall be filed with the following filing
5 officers:
6 (a) For candidates for national, state, or congressional office,
7 directors of public power and irrigation districts, directors of
8 reclamation districts, directors of natural resources districts,
9 directors of metropolitan utilities districts, members of the boards of
10 educational service units, members of governing boards of community
11 colleges, delegates to national conventions, and other offices filled by
12 election held in more than one county and judges desiring retention, in
13 the office of the Secretary of State;
14 (b) For officers elected within a county, in the office of the
15 election commissioner or county clerk;
16 (c) For officers in school districts which include land in adjoining
17 counties, in the office of the election commissioner or county clerk of
18 the county in which the greatest number of registered voters entitled to
19 vote for the officers reside; and
20 (d) For city or village officers, in the office of the election
21 commissioner or county clerk.
22 (3) Objections to the name of a candidate submitted on a candidate
23 filing form may be made and passed upon in the same manner as objections
24 to a candidate filing form pursuant to section 32-624.
25 Sec. 4. Original sections 23-3211 and 24-814, Reissue Revised
26 Statutes of Nebraska, and section 32-607, Revised Statutes Cumulative
27 Supplement, 2024, are repealed.
