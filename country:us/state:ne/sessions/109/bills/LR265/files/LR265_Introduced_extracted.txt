ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST SESSION
LEGISLATIVE RESOLUTION 265
Fredrickson, 20; Guereca, 7; Hunt, 8; Juarez, 5; McKinney, 11.
WHEREAS, John Ewing Jr. was the first Black mayor elected in the history
of Omaha, marking a significant milestone in the city's cultural legacy; and
WHEREAS, John has dedicated his life to public service, beginning with
over two decades in law enforcement with the Omaha Police Department, where he
rose to the rank of Deputy Chief and earned respect for his leadership and
integrity; and
WHEREAS, John was first elected Douglas County Treasurer in 2006,
modernizing the office and ensuring responsible stewardship of public funds;
and
WHEREAS, John is a proud graduate of the University of Nebraska at Omaha
and a lifelong community advocate, who has worked throughout his time in public
office to uplift underserved neighborhoods and expand economic opportunity; and
WHEREAS, John's historic victory as the first elected Black Mayor of Omaha
is a source of pride and inspiration for future generations of leaders,
especially young Black Nebraskans seeking to serve their communities.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE MEMBERS OF THE ONE HUNDRED NINTH
LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA, FIRST SESSION:

Section 2:
1. That the Legislature congratulates John Ewing Jr. on his election as
the first Black Mayor of Omaha and commends his decades of exemplary public
service.
