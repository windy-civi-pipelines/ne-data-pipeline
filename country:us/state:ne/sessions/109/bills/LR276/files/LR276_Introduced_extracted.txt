15.
WHEREAS, Andrew Ozaki, KETV's Lincoln Bureau Chief is retiring after
forty-three years of fair and objective reporting including decades here at the
Nebraska Legislature; and
WHEREAS, Andrew is a graduate of Lincoln High School and the University of
Nebraska-Lincoln, where he earned degrees in Political Science and Journalism;
and
WHEREAS, Andrew has spent his career working in journalism starting as an
intern for KETV in college, then for KOLN/KGIN News until he returned to KETV
in the Capitol Bureau; and
WHEREAS, Andrew's reporting has covered the Legislature and state
government for many years including eight Nebraska Governors, granting him
exceptional institutional knowledge; and
WHEREAS, Andrew was part of the reporting team to receive the prestigious
National Edward R. Murrow Award for their work on Chronicle: Mission to Africa;
and
WHEREAS, Andrew and his wife Margaret have raised three sons and now enjoy
four grandchildren. His family and community have all benefited from Andrew's
devotion and tireless work ethic; and
WHEREAS, now that Andrew is retiring, he leaves a legacy impacting future
community leaders and media here in Nebraska.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE MEMBERS OF THE ONE HUNDRED NINTH
