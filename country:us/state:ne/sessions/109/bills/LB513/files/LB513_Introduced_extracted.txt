LB513 LB513
2025 2025
LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA
ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST SESSION
Introduced by Bosn, 25.
Read first time January 21, 2025
Committee: Judiciary
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to judges' salaries; to amend section
2 24-201.01, Revised Statutes Cumulative Supplement, 2024; to change
3 judges' salaries; to provide an operative date; to repeal the
4 original section; and to declare an emergency.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB513 LB513
2025 2025
1 Section 1. Section 24-201.01, Revised Statutes Cumulative
2 Supplement, 2024, is amended to read:
3 24-201.01 On July 1, 2022, the salary of the Chief Justice and the
4 judges of the Supreme Court shall be one hundred ninety-eight thousand
5 four hundred twenty-six dollars and fifty-one cents. On July 1, 2023, the
6 salary of the Chief Justice and the judges of the Supreme Court shall be
7 two hundred twelve thousand three hundred sixteen dollars and thirty-
8 seven cents. On July 1, 2024, the salary of the Chief Justice and the
9 judges of the Supreme Court shall be two hundred twenty-five thousand
10 fifty-five dollars and thirty-five cents. On July 1, 2025, the salary of
11 the Chief Justice and the judges of the Supreme Court shall be two
12 hundred thirty-four thousand fifty-seven dollars and fifty-six cents. On
13 July 1, 2026, the salary of the Chief Justice and the judges of the
14 Supreme Court shall be two hundred forty-three thousand four hundred
15 nineteen dollars and eighty-seven cents.
16 The Chief Justice and the judges of the Supreme Court shall hold no
17 other public office of profit or trust during their terms of office nor
18 accept any public appointment or employment under the authority of the
19 government of the United States for which they receive compensation for
20 their services. Such salaries shall be payable in equal monthly
21 installments.
22 Sec. 2. This act becomes operative on July 1, 2025.
23 Sec. 3. Original section 24-201.01, Revised Statutes Cumulative
24 Supplement, 2024, is repealed.
25 Sec. 4. Since an emergency exists, this act takes effect when
26 passed and approved according to law.
