LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA
ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST SESSION
Introduced by Rountree, 3; Holdcroft, 36; Prokop, 27; Sanders, 45.
Read first time January 13, 2025
Committee: Business and Labor
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to labor; to amend sections 48-225, 48-226,
2 48-227, and 48-238, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to change
3 provisions relating to veterans preferences for public and private
4 employers; and to repeal the original sections.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-

2025 2025
1 Section 1. Section 48-225, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 48-225 For purposes of sections 48-225 to 48-231:
4 (1) Servicemember means a person who serves on active duty in the
5 armed forces of the United States except for training;
6 (2) Veteran means:
7 (a) A person who served full-time duty with military pay and
8 allowances in the armed forces of the United States, except for training
9 or for determining physical fitness, and was discharged or otherwise
10 separated with a characterization of honorable or general (under
11 honorable conditions); or
12 (b) The spouse of a veteran who (i) has a one hundred percent
13 permanent disability as determined by the United States Department of
14 Veterans Affairs or (ii) was killed in the line of duty or died due to
15 his or her military service;
16 (3) Full-time duty means duty during time of war or during a period
17 recognized by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs as
18 qualifying for veterans benefits administered by the department and that
19 such duty from January 31, 1955, to February 28, 1961, exceeded one
20 hundred eighty days unless lesser duty was the result of a service-
21 connected or service-aggravated disability;
22 (4) Disabled veteran means an individual who has served on active
23 duty in the armed forces of the United States, has been discharged or
24 otherwise separated with a characterization of honorable or general
25 (under honorable conditions) therefrom, and has established the present
26 existence of a service-connected disability or is receiving compensation,
27 disability retirement benefits, or pension because of a public statute
28 administered by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs or a
29 military department; and
30 (5) Preference eligible means any veteran as defined in this section
31 or the spouse of a servicemember as defined in this section, except that
-2-

1 for a spouse of a servicemember such preference is limited to the time
2 during which the servicemember remains serves on active status duty as
3 described in subdivision (1) of this section and up to one hundred eighty
4 days after the servicemember's discharge or separation from service;
5 and .
6 (6) Veterans preference means the preference given to preference
7 eligibles pursuant to sections 48-225 to 48-231.
8 Sec. 2. Section 48-226, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
9 amended to read:
10 48-226 A preference shall be given to preference eligibles seeking
11 employment with the State of Nebraska or its governmental subdivisions.
12 Such preference includes initial employment, promotion, reassignment, a
13 transfer to a new position, and or a return to employment with the State
14 of Nebraska or its governmental subdivisions if termination of previous
15 employment was for other than disciplinary reasons.
16 Sec. 3. Section 48-227, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
17 amended to read:
18 48-227 (1) Preference eligibles Veterans who obtain passing scores
19 on all parts or phases of an examination or numerical scoring shall have
20 five percent added to their passing score if a claim for such preference
21 is made on the application. An additional five percent shall be added to
22 the passing score or numerical scoring of any disabled veteran.
23 (2) When no examination or numerical scoring is used, the preference
24 shall be given to the preference eligible qualifying veteran if two or
25 more equally qualified candidates are being considered for the position.
26 (3) All notices of positions of employment available for veterans
27 preference and all applications for such positions by the state or its
28 governmental subdivisions shall state that the position is subject to a
29 veterans preference.
30 (4)(a) (4) A veteran desiring to use a veterans preference shall
31 provide the hiring authority with a copy of the veteran's Department of
-3-
LB144 LB144
2025 2025
1 Defense Form 214, also known as the DD Form 214, or its successor form or
2 record.
3 (b) A spouse of a veteran desiring to use a veterans preference
4 shall provide the hiring authority with (i) a copy of the veteran's
5 Department of Defense Form 214 or its successor form or record, (ii) a
6 copy of the veteran's disability verification from the United States
7 Department of Veterans Affairs demonstrating a one hundred percent
8 permanent disability rating or documentation showing the veteran was
9 killed in the line of duty or died due to his or her military service,
10 whichever is applicable , and (iii) proof of marriage to the veteran.
11 (c) A spouse of a servicemember desiring to use a veterans
12 preference shall provide the hiring authority with proof of the
13 servicemember's active status and proof of marriage to the servicemember.
14 (d) Any marriage claimed for veterans preference must be valid under
15 Nebraska law.
16 (5) Within thirty days after filling a position, preference
17 eligibles veterans who have applied and are not hired shall be notified
18 by regular mail, electronic mail, telephone call, or personal service
19 that they have not been hired. Such notice also shall advise the
20 preference eligible veteran of any administrative appeal available.
21 Sec. 4. Section 48-238, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
22 amended to read:
23 48-238 (1) For purposes of this section:
24 (a) Preference eligible means any veteran as defined in this section
25 or the spouse of a servicemember as defined in this section, except that
26 for a spouse of a servicemember such preference is limited to the time
27 during which the servicemember remains on active status as described in
28 subdivision (1)(c) of this section and up to one hundred eighty days
29 after the servicemember's discharge or separation from service;
30 (b) (a) Private employer means a sole proprietorship, a corporation,
31 a partnership, an association, a limited liability company, or any other

1 entity with one or more employees;
2 (c) Servicemember means a person who serves in the armed forces of
3 the United States;
4 (d) (b) Veteran means (i) a person who served full-time duty with
5 military pay and allowances in the armed forces of the United States,
6 except for training or for determining physical fitness, and was
7 discharged or otherwise separated with a characterization of honorable or
8 general (under honorable conditions), or (ii) the spouse of a veteran who
9 (A) has a one hundred percent permanent disability as determined by the
10 United States Department of Veterans Affairs or (B) was killed in the
11 line of duty or died due to his or her military service hostile action;
12 and
13 (e) (c) Voluntary veterans preference employment policy means a
14 private employer's voluntary preference for hiring and promoting a
15 preference eligible veteran over another equally qualified applicant or
16 employee.
17 (2) A private employer may adopt a voluntary veterans preference
18 employment policy. Such policy shall be in writing and applied uniformly
19 to decisions regarding hiring and promotion.
20 (3) If a private employer offers a voluntary veterans preference
21 employment policy: ,
22 (a) A a veteran desiring to use such policy shall provide the
23 private employer with a copy of the veteran's Department of Defense Form
24 214, also known as the DD Form 214, or its successor form or record; .
25 (b) A spouse of a veteran desiring to use such policy preference
26 shall provide the private employer with a copy of the veteran's
27 Department of Defense Form 214 or its successor form or record, proof of
28 marriage to the veteran, and (i) either (a) a copy of the veteran's
29 disability verification from the United States Department of Veterans
30 Affairs demonstrating a one hundred percent permanent disability rating,
31 (ii) or (b) a copy of the veteran's Department of Defense Form 1300 or
-5-
LB144 LB144
2025 2025
1 its successor form documenting that the veteran was killed in the line of
2 duty, or (iii) other documentation showing that the veteran died due to
3 his or her military service; and hostile action.
4 (c) A spouse of a servicemember desiring to use such policy shall
5 provide the private employer with proof of the servicemember's active
6 status and proof of marriage to the servicemember.
7 (4) If a private employer implements a voluntary veterans preference
8 employment policy, it shall notify the Commissioner of Labor of such
9 policy. The commissioner shall use the information to maintain a registry
10 of the private employers that have a voluntary veterans preference
11 employment policy in Nebraska.
12 (5) A voluntary veterans preference employment policy shall not be
13 considered a violation of any state or local equal employment opportunity
14 law including the Nebraska Fair Employment Practice Act.
15 Sec. 5. Original sections 48-225, 48-226, 48-227, and 48-238,
16 Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, are repealed.
