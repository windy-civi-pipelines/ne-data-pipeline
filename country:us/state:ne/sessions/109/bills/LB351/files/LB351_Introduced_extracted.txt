1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to state hospitals for the mentally ill; to
2 amend section 83-338, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska; to
3 change requirements for priority of admission; and to repeal the
4 original section.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB351 LB351
2025 2025
1 Section 1. Section 83-338, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 83-338 (1) State hospitals for the mentally ill shall prioritize the
4 following patients for admission If at any time it becomes necessary, for
5 lack of capacity or other cause, to establish priorities for the
6 admission of patients into the state hospitals for the mentally ill,
7 admission shall be limited to: (a) Patients whose care in the state
8 hospital is necessary in order to protect the public health and safety;
9 (b) defendants who are determined by a court to be incompetent to stand
10 trial and who remain lodged in the county jail; and (c) patients
11 committed by a mental health board under the Nebraska Mental Health
12 Commitment Act or the Sex Offender Commitment Act, by a district court,
13 or by a tribal court as provided in section 71-964 or 71-1226.01 ; (d)
14 patients who are most likely to be benefited by treatment in the state
15 hospitals, regardless of whether such patients are committed by a mental
16 health board or whether such patients seek voluntary admission to one of
17 the state hospitals; and (e) when cases are equally meritorious, in all
18 other respects, patients who are indigent.
19 (2) The Lincoln Regional Center shall provide a minimum number of
20 beds equal to the following percentages of total available capacity:
21 (a) Ten percent for patients entering the facility pursuant to
22 subdivision (1)(a) of this section;
23 (b) Ten percent for patients entering the facility pursuant to
24 subdivision (1)(b) of this section;
25 (c) Thirty percent for patients entering the facility pursuant to
26 subdivision (1)(c) of this section;
27 (d) Ten percent for patients entering the facility pursuant to
28 subdivision (1)(d) of this section;
29 (e) Ten percent for patients entering the facility pursuant to
30 subdivision (1)(e) of this section; and
31 (f) Thirty percent to remain unallocated for the Department of
-2-
LB351 LB351
2025 2025
1 Health and Human Services to allocate according to the priorities
2 established in subsection (1) of this section as needed to reduce
3 existing waiting lists.
4 (2) (3) Beginning on or before November 1, 2022, the Department of
5 Health and Human Services shall make available on its website a weekly
6 report on the capacity and status of state-operated mental health
7 facilities, including, but not limited to, the capacity and wait times
8 associated with each priority category established in subsection (1) of
9 this section.
10 Sec. 2. Original section 83-338, Reissue Revised Statutes of
11 Nebraska, is repealed.
