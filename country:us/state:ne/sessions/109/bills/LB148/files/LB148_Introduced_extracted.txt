LEGISLATURE OF NEBRASKA
ONE HUNDRED NINTH LEGISLATURE
FIRST SESSION
Introduced by Hansen, 16.
Read first time January 13, 2025
Committee: Health and Human Services
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to the Dentistry Practice Act; to amend
2 sections 38-1117 and 38-1120, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska;
3 to change requirements for licensure and reciprocity as prescribed;
4 and to repeal the original sections.
5 Be it enacted by the people of the State of Nebraska,
-1-
LB148 LB148
2025 2025
1 Section 1. Section 38-1117, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
2 amended to read:
3 38-1117 (1) Every applicant for a license to practice dentistry
4 shall (a) present proof of graduation with a Doctor of Dental Surgery
5 degree or a Doctor of Dental Medicine degree from an accredited school or
6 college of dentistry, (b) pass an examination approved by the Board of
7 Dentistry which shall consist of the Integrated National Board Dental
8 Examination or Examinations, both Part I and Part II of the National
9 Board Dental Examination, as constructed and administered by the American
10 Dental Association Joint Commission on National Dental Examinations, (c)
11 demonstrate the applicant's psychomotor skill in clinical dentistry by
12 passing a standardized national examination approved by the practical
13 examination administered by the Central Regional Dental Testing Service
14 or any other regional or state practical examination that the board Board
15 of Dentistry determines to be comparable to such practical examination,
16 (d) pass a jurisprudence examination approved by the board that is based
17 on the Nebraska statutes, rules, and regulations governing the practice
18 of dentistry and dental hygiene, and (e) demonstrate continuing clinical
19 competency as a condition of licensure if required by the board.
20 (2)(a) An examination approved by the board under subdivision (1)(c)
21 of this section shall be a simulation-based or manikin-based clinical
22 competency psychomotor examination that demonstrates evidence of
23 psychometric soundness and shall include (i) a simulation-based or
24 manikin-based periodontal component, (ii) a simulation-based or manikin-
25 based restorative component, (iii) a simulation-based or manikin-based
26 prosthodontic component, (iv) a simulation-based or manikin-based
27 endodontic component, and (v) a remediation policy to address candidate
28 deficiencies.
29 (b) The board may review examinations annually and shall review
30 examinations at least every three years.
31 (3) (2) Upon completion of these requirements, the department, with
-2-
LB148 LB148
2025 2025
1 the recommendation of the board, shall issue the applicant a license to
2 practice dentistry.
3 Sec. 2. Section 38-1120, Reissue Revised Statutes of Nebraska, is
4 amended to read:
5 38-1120 Every applicant for a license to practice dentistry based on
6 a license in another state or territory of the United States or the
7 District of Columbia shall meet the standards set by the board pursuant
8 to section 38-126 and shall have been actively engaged in the practice of
9 dentistry for at least one year three years, one of which must be within
10 the three years immediately preceding the application, under a license in
11 another state or territory of the United States or the District of
12 Columbia. Practice in an accredited school or college of dentistry for
13 the purpose of completing a postgraduate or residency program in
14 dentistry also serves as active practice toward meeting this requirement.
15 Sec. 3. Original sections 38-1117 and 38-1120, Reissue Revised
16 Statutes of Nebraska, are repealed.
